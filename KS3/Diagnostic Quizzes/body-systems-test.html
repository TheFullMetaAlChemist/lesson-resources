Question 1a

Identifying organs

Question
Circle the two organs from the list.

Model answer

Heart and stomach.

Explanation

An organ is made of different tissues working together to do a job. The heart and stomach are organs. Systems and cells are not organs.

Common mistakes

  • Choosing the respiratory or circulatory system
  • Choosing red blood cell, egg cell or sperm cell
Question 1b

Why the digestive system is an organ system

Question
Explain why the digestive system is classed as an organ system.

Model answer

It is a group of organs working together to carry out a function, which is digestion.

Explanation

An organ system is not just one organ. It is several organs, each with a role, working together to complete one bigger job.

Common mistakes

  • Saying it is an organ system because it is inside the body
  • Saying all the organs do exactly the same job
  • Naming organs without explaining that they work together
Question 2

Gas exchange in humans

Question
Describe how the process of gas exchange is carried out in humans.

Model answer

Gas exchange happens in the lungs. Oxygen moves from the air in the lungs into the blood, and carbon dioxide moves from the blood into the lungs to be breathed out. This happens by diffusion. The oxygen is then carried to body cells.

Explanation

To reach full marks, you needed both gases, the correct direction of movement, at least one organ or tissue, and the process of diffusion.

Common mistakes

  • Only mentioning oxygen
  • Getting the direction wrong
  • Saying gas exchange happens in the heart
  • Forgetting the word diffusion
What made this a 4-mark answer?
A top answer did not just say “oxygen goes into the body”. It explained where gas exchange happens, which gases move, where they move to and from, and named diffusion as the process.
Question 3a(i)

Bell jar model: the balloons

Question
In the bell jar model, what do the balloons represent?

Model answer

The lungs.

Explanation

The balloons inflate and deflate like the lungs during breathing.

Common mistakes

  • Saying diaphragm
  • Saying rib cage
  • Trying to be too detailed and saying alveoli
Question 3a(ii)

Bell jar model: the rubber sheet

Question
In the bell jar model, what does the flexible rubber sheet represent?

Model answer

The diaphragm.

Explanation

When the sheet moves, it changes the space inside the bell jar in the same way the diaphragm changes the space in the chest.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing it up with the lungs
  • Saying it represents the ribs
Question 3b

What happens during inhalation

Question
Complete the sentences using the words given.

Model answer

When we inhale, the rib cage moves up and out.
At the same time, the diaphragm moves down.

Explanation

These movements make the chest cavity bigger, helping air move into the lungs.

Common mistakes

  • Confusing inhalation with exhalation
  • Saying the diaphragm moves up when breathing in
  • Saying the ribs move down and in
Question 3c(i)

How the model works well

Question
Describe one way the model represents inhalation and exhalation accurately.

Model answer

Moving the rubber sheet causes air to move in and out of the balloons, like the diaphragm causes air to move in and out of the lungs.

Explanation

A good answer links the part being moved to the effect it causes. It is not enough just to say “it shows breathing”.

Common mistakes

  • Giving a vague answer with no clear link
  • Describing appearance instead of function
Question 3c(ii)

How the model is limited

Question
Describe one way the model does not represent inhalation and exhalation accurately.

Model answer

The rib cage does not move in the model, but a real rib cage moves during breathing.

Explanation

This is the main weakness of the bell jar model. It shows the diaphragm effect fairly well, but not rib movement.

Common mistakes

  • Talking about the model being made of glass
  • Giving a point that is not about how breathing is modelled
Question 3d

Why air moves into the lungs

Question
Explain one change during inhalation, other than the one in part b, that causes air to move into the lungs.

Model answer

The muscles between the ribs contract, so the rib cage moves up and out. This increases the volume of the chest cavity and lowers the pressure, so air moves into the lungs.

Explanation

This question wanted more than a movement. It wanted the reason that movement makes air enter: bigger chest space means lower pressure.

Common mistakes

  • Repeating only that the diaphragm moves down
  • Not mentioning pressure or volume
  • Saying pressure increases
Exam tip: For “explain” questions, try to build a chain: muscle movement → chest change → pressure change → air movement.

Question 4b(i)

Ball and socket joints

Question
Name one part of the body that contains a ball and socket joint.

Model answer

Hip or shoulder.

Explanation

Ball and socket joints allow movement in many directions.

Common mistakes

  • Saying knee or elbow
  • Naming a bone instead of a joint location
Question 4b(ii)

Hinge joints

Question
Name one part of the body that contains a hinge joint.

Model answer

Knee or elbow.

Explanation

Hinge joints mainly allow movement backwards and forwards in one plane.

Common mistakes

  • Saying hip or shoulder
  • Giving a very vague answer such as “leg”
Question 4b(iii)

Why arthritis causes pain

Question
Suggest why arthritis can cause pain during movement.

Model answer

Cartilage normally reduces friction between bones. If cartilage is lost, the bones rub together, which causes pain when the joint moves.

Explanation

The key science here is that cartilage protects the joint and reduces friction. Without it, movement becomes painful.

Common mistakes

  • Saying the bones break
  • Talking only about swelling without explaining rubbing bones
  • Saying muscles rub together
Question 5a

Main function of muscle

Question
Give the main function of muscle in the body.

Model answer

Movement.

Explanation

The mark scheme also accepts locomotion. The question wanted the main job muscles do.

Common mistakes

  • Giving a vague answer such as “help the body”
  • Naming a specific example instead of the main function
Question 5b

How muscles cause movement

Question
Explain how a muscle causes movement in the body.

Model answer

A muscle contracts and gets shorter. It pulls on a bone, and the muscle is attached to the bone by tendons.

Explanation

All three points were needed for full marks: contraction, pulling on bone, and tendons attaching muscle to bone.

Common mistakes

  • Saying muscles push bones
  • Forgetting tendons
  • Saying bones pull muscles
Question 5c

Measuring the force of the biceps

Question
Describe how to measure the force that the biceps can exert.

Model answer

Use a known mass or weight, or a force meter/newton meter. Lift the weight by bending the arm at the elbow using the biceps. Record the force and use it to work out the force exerted by the biceps, remembering that the force at the hand is not exactly the same as the force produced by the muscle.

Explanation

This answer needed equipment, a workable method, and some recognition that the force measured at the hand is not exactly the same as the force from the biceps.

Common mistakes

  • Not naming any equipment
  • Giving no actual method
  • Assuming the muscle force is exactly equal to the weight lifted
How could you improve this answer?
Add more detail about keeping the method workable and measurable, for example using a known weight, lifting it in a controlled way, and recording the reading carefully.
Question 5d

Evaluating the muscle force method

Question
Evaluate how well this method gives a valid result. Include one advantage and one limitation.

Model answer

Advantage: The weight used is known and can be measured accurately.

Limitation: More than one muscle is working, or the two arms may not use the same effort, so the result may not be fully valid.

Explanation

This question was about judging the method. A good answer includes one strength and one weakness, not just a description of what happens.

Common mistakes

  • Giving two advantages or two limitations
  • Ignoring that other muscles may help
  • Saying “it is fair” without explaining why
Final reflection: The most common pattern in this test was not explaining enough. When you see words like describe, explain or evaluate, make sure your answer does the full job.
