Metals and Non-metals: An Interactive Lesson

1. Introduction to Metals and Non-metals

Elements in the periodic table can be broadly classified as metals or non-metals. Metals are typically found on the left side and center of the periodic table, while non-metals are often on the right side.

Metals have characteristic physical properties like being good conductors of heat and electricity, having high melting points, and being malleable or ductile. Non-metals, on the other hand, tend to be brittle (when solid), have lower melting points, and are poor conductors of heat and electricity.

When metals react with oxygen, they form metal oxides. Most metal oxides are basic oxides, meaning they can react with acids to form salts and water.

4. Oxidation Reactions & Particle Diagrams

An oxidation reaction is one in which a substance combines with oxygen. We often show these reactions with particle diagrams to illustrate how atoms rearrange to form new substances.

For instance, magnesium atoms (Mg) can combine with oxygen molecules (O2) to form magnesium oxide (MgO). In a particle diagram, you’d see two magnesium atoms bonding with two oxygen atoms to produce two MgO units.
