Today's big idea

White light contains a range of colours. Materials can transmit, absorb or reflect different parts of the light that reaches them.

1. Split white light with a prism

Explore dispersion and compare the refraction of different colours.

Prism demonstrationViolet refracts more than red

Red lightlower frequency · refracts less

Predict before revealing

Which colour will change direction the most? Would a single red beam split into a rainbow?
Dispersion: white light contains a continuous range of colours. Violet light is refracted more than red light, so the colours spread out into a spectrum. A single colour refracts but does not split into a rainbow.
Key words: spectrum · continuous · dispersion · refraction · frequency

Predict before adjusting

What appears when red and green light overlap? How could you make orange rather than yellow?
Watch out: mixing coloured light is not the same as mixing paint. Adding more light makes the result brighter.
Additive mixing: red + green makes yellow, green + blue makes cyan, and blue + red makes magenta. Equal red, green and blue makes white. Orange can be made using more red than green.
Key words: primary colour · secondary colour · additive mixing · intensity

Filters subtract colours. A red filter transmits red light and absorbs the other components. Secondary filters transmit two components: cyan transmits green and blue, magenta transmits red and blue, and yellow transmits red and green.
Key words: filter · transmit · absorb · subtract · available light

4. Explain why objects have colours

Change the light source, add an optional filter and track reflected light into the eye.

What does the observer see?

Predict the appearance before looking at the reflected-light arrow.
Red apple appears red

The apple reflects red light into the eye and absorbs most of the other colours.

Objects do not create their colour. They appear coloured because they reflect some of the available light and absorb the rest. White objects reflect the colours available. Black objects reflect very little visible light.
Key words: reflect · absorb · available light · appearance
