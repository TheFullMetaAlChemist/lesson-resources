Unicellular Organisms

Year 7 Reading Comprehension Worksheet
Instructions for students: Read the textbook page about unicellular organisms, amoebas and euglenas carefully. Answer each question in full sentences using scientific language. When you have finished, you may click on each “Show answer” drop-down to check your work or get help if you are stuck.

Section A – Basic Recall

1. What is a unicellular organism?

Show answer

A unicellular organism is a living thing that is made of just one cell.

2. Name two places where amoebas can be found.

Show answer

Amoebas can be found in places such as fresh water, salt water, wet soil and even inside animals (any two of these).

3. What does the nucleus do in an amoeba?

Show answer

The nucleus controls the amoeba’s growth and reproduction and also controls the cell’s activities.

4. How does an amoeba move?

Show answer

An amoeba moves by changing the shape of its body, pushing part of the cell out in the direction it wants to go and letting the rest of the cell flow into it.

5. What is the function of the food vacuole in an amoeba?

Show answer

The food vacuole stores particles of food and digests them so the amoeba can use the nutrients.

6. Why does an amoeba need a contractile vacuole?

Show answer

The contractile vacuole removes excess water (and some waste) from the cell so that the amoeba does not swell up and burst.

7. What is the process called when an amoeba splits to form two new identical cells?

Show answer

The process is called binary fission.

Section B – Euglena Features

8. Name the structure that helps a euglena detect light.

Show answer

The euglena has an eye spot that helps it detect light.

9. What is the role of the chloroplasts in a euglena?

Show answer

The chloroplasts absorb light energy so the euglena can carry out photosynthesis and make its own food.

10. What is the flagellum used for?

Show answer

The flagellum is a tail-like structure that beats like a propeller and helps the euglena swim through the water, often towards light.

11. Give one way in which a euglena is different from an amoeba.

Show answer

One difference is that a euglena has chloroplasts and can make its own food by photosynthesis, while an amoeba does not have chloroplasts and cannot photosynthesise.

Section C – Understanding & Application

12. Amoebas and euglenas are unicellular. Explain what this means using your own words.

Show answer

This means each organism is made of a single cell, and that one cell carries out all the life processes needed for the organism to survive.

13. A student says that a euglena is “like both a plant and an animal.” Using what you’ve read, explain why this claim could be partly true.

Show answer

The euglena is plant-like because it has chloroplasts and can photosynthesise to make its own food. It is also animal-like because it can move using a flagellum and can engulf and digest other organisms when there is not enough light.

14. Why is binary fission a useful method of reproduction for unicellular organisms?

Show answer

Binary fission is useful because it is a quick way to produce two identical daughter cells from one parent cell. The organism does not need a mate and can increase its numbers rapidly.

Section D – Challenge Questions

15. Explain how the contractile vacuole helps prevent an amoeba or euglena from bursting.

Show answer

The contractile vacuole collects extra water inside the cell and then squeezes it out. This keeps the water level inside the cell balanced so the cell membrane does not stretch too much and burst.

16. How do the structures of a euglena help it to survive in water where light levels change?

Show answer

The eye spot helps the euglena sense where light is coming from, and the flagellum lets it swim towards brighter light so the chloroplasts can photosynthesise. If there is not enough light, the euglena can feed by surrounding and digesting other small organisms.

17. If a euglena cannot make enough food by photosynthesis, what does it do to survive? Why is this useful?

Show answer

When it cannot make enough food by photosynthesis, a euglena feeds like an animal by surrounding and engulfing bacteria or algae and digesting them. This is useful because it allows the euglena to survive even when light levels are low.
