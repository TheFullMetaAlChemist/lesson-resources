Q1

First order with respect to propanone — which graph?

Graphs
Answer: The diagnostic plot is ln[propanone] vs time as a straight line with gradient −k (or a concentration–time curve with a constant half-life). Match to the option showing a straight-line decay of ln[C] with t.
Why / Thought process

For a first-order reactant A: ln[A] = −k·t + ln[A]₀ — a straight line in ln[A] vs t with slope −k. A straight line in 1/[A] vs t indicates second order; a straight line in [A] vs t indicates zero order.

Tips
  • Constant half-life in the raw [A]–t curve → first order.
  • Diagnostics: [A] vs t linear = zero; ln[A] vs t linear = first; 1/[A] vs t linear = second.

Q2

Zero order in W, first order in X: rate when [X] doubles?

Orders
Answer: B. 1.4 × 10⁻³ mol dm⁻³ s⁻¹
Why / Thought process

Rate ∝ [X]. Doubling X from 0.50 → 1.00 doubles the rate: 2 × 7.0 × 10⁻⁴ = 1.4 × 10⁻³. W is zero order, so changing W does nothing.

Tips

If a reactant is zero order, its concentration changes do not affect rate (though it can appear in the stoichiometry).

Q3

Units of k for rate = k[X][Y]

Units
Answer: D. dm³ mol⁻¹ s⁻¹
Why / Thought process

Rate units: mol dm⁻³ s⁻¹. The term [X][Y] has units (mol dm⁻³)². Hence k = (mol dm⁻³ s⁻¹) ÷ (mol² dm⁻⁶) = dm³ mol⁻¹ s⁻¹.

Tips

General (overall order n): units(k) = dm³⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾ mol¹⁻ⁿ s⁻¹.

Arrhenius terms — which has incorrect units?

Arrhenius
Answer: B (the unit for R is wrong on the option sheet)
Why / Thought process

Correct units: Eₐ: J mol⁻¹; R: J K⁻¹ mol⁻¹; T: K; e: none. The K⁻¹ on R is essential.

Q5

From an Arrhenius plot of ln k vs 1/T, what can you obtain?

Arrhenius
Answer: A. Activation energy Eₐ
Why / Thought process

Gradient m = −Eₐ/R. From m and R compute Eₐ. The intercept gives ln A.

Tips

If the question mentions the intercept, that is ln A (the pre-exponential factor).

Q6

Zero-order concentration–time graph

Graphs
Answer: The correct graph is a straight line decrease of [reactant] with time at a constant rate (linear down to zero).
Why / Thought process

Zero order: [A] = [A]₀ − k·t. Linear fall; slope magnitude is k. The half-life decreases as [A] drops (not constant).

Tips

Compare half-lives: decreasing t½ → zero order; constant t½ → first order.

Q7

Iodine–propanone (acid-catalysed): rate law k[H⁺][C₃H₆O]

Orders
Answer: C. Iodine: zero; Propanone: first; Sulfuric acid: first.
Why / Thought process

Species in the rate law carry non-zero order; species absent are zero order. Hence first order in propanone and H⁺; zero order in I₂.

Tips

A reactant can be zero order if it reacts after the rate-determining step or is in fast steps.

Q9

Rate–concentration graphs for a second-order reactant

Graphs
Answer: Choose the graph where rate vs [A] is a curve through the origin that gets steeper (quadratic), or where rate vs [A]² is a straight line through the origin.
Why / Thought process

For second order in A: rate = k[A]². Plotting rate vs [A] gives a curve; plotting rate vs [A]² linearises it.

Tips

First order → rate vs [A] is linear; zero order → horizontal line (rate independent of [A]).

Q10

Arrhenius slope −45000: what is Eₐ?

Arrhenius
Answer: A. 374 kJ mol⁻¹
Why / Thought process

For ln k vs 1/T, slope m = −Eₐ/R. So Eₐ = (−m)R = 45000 × 8.31 ≈ 3.74 × 10⁵ J mol⁻¹ = 374 kJ mol⁻¹.

Tips

Keep R and Eₐ in J to avoid rounding issues; convert to kJ at the end.

Q11

From Arrhenius plot (N₂O₅ decomposition), find A

Arrhenius
Answer: D. 4.79 × 10¹³ s⁻¹
Why / Thought process

On ln k vs 1/T, the intercept is ln A. Exponentiating the graph’s intercept yields A ≈ 10¹³ s⁻¹ (closest option 4.79 × 10¹³).

Ensure you’re reading the intercept (not a data point). Units of A match those of k for the order (often s⁻¹).

Q12

Acidified propanone + iodine: compute k for rate = k[acetone][H⁺]

Calculations
Answer: B. 1.13 × 10⁻³ dm³ mol⁻¹ s⁻¹
Why / Thought process

k = rate ÷ ([CH₃COCH₃] · [H⁺]) ≈ (2.75 × 10⁻³) ÷ (3.32 × 0.73) ≈ 1.13 × 10⁻³. Iodine doesn’t appear in the rate law → ignore it when computing k.

Overall order 2 → units of k are dm³ mol⁻¹ s⁻¹.

Q13

Activation energy from (1/T, ln k) data

Arrhenius
Answer: C. 90.7 kJ mol⁻¹
Why / Thought process

Fit ln k = m(1/T) + c -> m = −Eₐ/R. With gradients around −1.10 × 10⁴, Eₐ ≈ (1.10 × 10⁴) × 8.31 = 9.1 × 10⁴ J mol⁻¹ = 90.7 kJ mol⁻¹.

Q14

Deduce the rate equation from experiment table

Orders
Answer: A. rate = k[A][C]²
Why / Thought process
  1. 1 -> 2: A and B both doubled, C same; rate only doubles -> combined effect ×2 -> first order in A, zero in B (or vice versa).
  2. 1 -> 3: C halves; rate quarters -> second order in C.
  3. 1 -> 4: A ×3 and C ×2 -> rate ×(3 × 2²) = ×12, matching the data. So rate = k[A][C]².
Tips

Change one variable at a time when possible; use a final row as a consistency check.

Q15

NO + H₂ mechanism; which statements are true?

Mechanisms
Answer: B. Only 1 and 2 are true
Why / Thought process

Observed rate: rate = k[NO]²[H₂]. If a fast pre-equilibrium 2 NO <-> N₂O₂ precedes a rate-determining step N₂O₂ + H₂ -> …, the rate law matches. Halving [NO] and doubling [H₂] gives (½)² × 2 = ½ -> rate halves. Statement 3 is false: k depends on temperature via Arrhenius.

Tips

Intermediates/catalysts can appear in the mechanism but not in the overall equation; the RDS and any pre-equilibria determine the rate law form.
