Show calculation notes

[I⁻] = concentration of KI × volume of KI ÷ total volume.

Rate = 2.00 × 10⁻³ ÷ time.

Clock point: 2 mol S₂O₃²⁻ reacts with 1 mol I₂, and formation of 1 mol I₂ corresponds to disappearance of 2 mol I⁻. Therefore the amount of I⁻ used up at the clock point is numerically equal to the amount of thiosulfate originally present.

Initial rate = concentration change of I⁻ ÷ time = 2.00 × 10⁻³ ÷ t.

For a first-order iodide graph: gradient of full rate graph = k[S₂O₈²⁻], so k = gradient ÷ [S₂O₈²⁻].
