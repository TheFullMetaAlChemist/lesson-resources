Core Essentials for Electrolysis

  • Electrolysis: Splitting an ionic compound (liquid or dissolved) using electricity.
  • Electrolyte: The ionic compound that is being broken down (must be molten or in solution so ions can move).
  • Electrodes: Rods that conduct electricity into the electrolyte.
    • Anode: POSITIVE electrode. Anions (negative ions) go here. OXIDATION happens here. (Remember: PANIC - Positive Anode, Negative Is Cathode; ANOX - Anode Oxidation)
    • Cathode: NEGATIVE electrode. Cations (positive ions) go here. REDUCTION happens here. (Remember: RED CAT - Reduction at Cathode)
  • Ions: Charged particles.
    • Cations: POSITIVE ions (e.g., Na‚Å∫, Cu¬≤‚Å∫, H‚Å∫). Attracted to the CATHODE.
    • Anions: NEGATIVE ions (e.g., Cl‚Åª, O¬≤‚Åª, OH‚Åª). Attracted to the ANODE.
  • OILRIG: Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain (of electrons).

Activity 3: Aqueous Compounds ‚Äì Predicting Products

Key Rules for Aqueous Solutions:

  • Aqueous: Means the ionic compound is DISSOLVED IN WATER.
  • Water itself provides some ions: H‚Å∫ and OH‚Åª. So, you'll always have these two ions in addition to the ions from the dissolved compound.
  • At the CATHODE (-): (Positive ions: Metal ion from salt AND H‚Å∫ from water are attracted)
    • If the metal from the salt is MORE reactive than Hydrogen ‚Üí **HYDROGEN GAS (H‚ÇÇ)** is produced.
    • If the metal from the salt is LESS reactive than Hydrogen ‚Üí **THE METAL** itself is produced.
  • At the ANODE (+): (Negative ions: Non-metal ion from salt AND OH‚Åª from water are attracted)
    • If **HALIDE ions** (Cl‚Åª, Br‚Åª, I‚Åª from the salt) are present AND the solution is **concentrated** ‚Üí The **HALOGEN (Cl‚ÇÇ, Br‚ÇÇ, I‚ÇÇ)** is produced.
