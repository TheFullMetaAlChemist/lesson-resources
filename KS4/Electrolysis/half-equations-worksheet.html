Writing Half-Equations for Electrolysis

Electrolysis is used to extract metals from molten compounds. During electrolysis:

  • Positive metal ions go to the cathode (negative electrode) and gain electrons (reduction).
  • Negative non-metal ions go to the anode (positive electrode) and lose electrons (oxidation). A half-equation shows what happens at just one electrode. You must balance both the atoms and the charge by adding the correct number of electrons (e-).

Use the following steps to write half-equations:

  1. Identify the ion involved.
  2. Write the product (metal atom or non-metal molecule).
  3. Balance the atoms.
  4. Balance the charges with electrons.
