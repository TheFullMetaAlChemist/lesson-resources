Reactions of Acids: Writing Chemical Equations

General Equations

  1. Metal + Acid: Metal + Acid → Salt + Hydrogen
    • Metals react with acids to form a salt and hydrogen gas. The salt’s name comes from the metal and the acid used.
    • Example: Magnesium + Hydrochloric Acid → Magnesium Chloride + Hydrogen
  2. Acid + Alkali: Acid + Alkali → Salt + Water
    • This is a neutralization reaction. The acid and alkali react to form a salt and water.
    • Example: Hydrochloric Acid + Sodium Hydroxide → Sodium Chloride + Water
  3. Acid + Metal Oxide: Acid + Metal Oxide → Salt + Water
    • Metal oxides are bases and react with acids to form a salt and water.
    • Example: Sulfuric Acid + Copper(II) Oxide → Copper(II) Sulfate + Water
  4. Acid + Carbonate: Acid + Carbonate → Salt + Water + Carbon Dioxide
    • Carbonates react with acids to produce a salt, water, and carbon dioxide gas.
    • Example: Hydrochloric Acid + Calcium Carbonate → Calcium Chloride + Water + Carbon Dioxide
