Key formula

moles = mass ÷ Mr

Use this whenever the question gives a mass in grams.

Common ratio conversions

After dividingMultiply byFinal ratio
1 : 1.5×22 : 3
1 : 2.5×22 : 5
1 : 1.33×33 : 4

Important exam distinction

When selecting a balanced equation: compare the mole ratio of the reactants and match it to the coefficients in the options.

When finding a product formula: compare the atom ratio in the product, then use that ratio to build the formula.

Common mistake warning

Do not just guess the formula from chemistry knowledge. In these questions, the examiner wants you to use the data to calculate the ratio.

Common mistakes checklist

Tick each one once you understand how to avoid it.

☐ I converted masses into moles before using ratios.

☐ I multiplied ratios such as 1 : 1.5 to make whole numbers.

☐ I remembered that oxygen, chlorine, bromine and hydrogen are diatomic in equations.

☐ I know the difference between finding a product formula and selecting a balanced equation.
