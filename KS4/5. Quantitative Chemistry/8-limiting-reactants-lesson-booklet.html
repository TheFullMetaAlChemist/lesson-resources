Introduction: Limiting and Excess Reactants

In chemical reactions, reactants combine in fixed ratios to make products. Sometimes one reactant runs out before the others.

Key Concept

The limiting reactant is the substance that is completely used up and limits the amount of product that can be made.

Understanding and Using Mole Ratios

Worked Example: Reading the Ratio

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
H₂ : O₂ : H₂O = 2 : 1 : 2
  • 2 moles of hydrogen react with 1 mole of oxygen.
  • 2 moles of water are produced.
  • The ratio can be scaled up or down.

Finding the Limiting Reactant Using Moles

Method

  1. Write the mole ratio from the equation.
  2. Divide actual moles by the ratio.
  3. The smallest value is the limiting reactant.

Worked Example

Method
