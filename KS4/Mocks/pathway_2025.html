Q02

Hydrocarbon Fuels / Fractional Distillation

02.1 Fractional Distillation

Why is it used?

Reveal Model Answer & Tips

Model Answer

Crude oil is heated and its vapours are separated into fractions based on different boiling points.

Exam Tip

If you see a tall column with trays and a heater — the answer is fractional distillation.

Revision Tip

Different hydrocarbons = different boiling points → separate as they condense at different heights.

02.2 Change of state

Identify the change of state happening in the Heater vs the Column.

Reveal Model Answer & Tips

Model Answer

Location Model Answer Explanation
Heater Evaporation / Vaporisation Hydrocarbons boil and turn into gases
Column Condensation Hydrocarbons cool and turn back to liquids

Exam Tip

  • Evaporation happens where heat is added → bottom
  • Condensation happens where it’s cooler → higher up the column

02.4 Flammability

State the correct order of increasing flammability.

Reveal Answer

Model Answer

➡️ Heavy fuel oil → diesel oil → kerosene

Why? Smaller molecules ignite more easily → more flammable → lower boiling point.

Exam Tip

Flammability trend is opposite to boiling point & molecule size.

Smaller = easier to burn.

Catalysts & Rates (H₂O₂ Decomposition)

08.1 Mass Changes

Why does the mass decrease?

Reveal Answer

Model Answer

Extra explanation for 2 marks: The balance detects a loss in mass because the gas leaves the system.

Exam Tip

Gas leaving the system = mass decreases.

08.2 Reaction Profile

Compare Catalyst A and B.

Reveal Model Answer (3 Marks)

Model Answer

  1. Catalyst B lowers the activation energy more than catalyst A.
  2. More particles have energy ≥ activation energy with catalyst B.
  3. So, the reaction with catalyst B occurs faster.

Exam Tip

Use key phrase: “Lowers the activation energy”

Revision Tip

Catalysts never change:

  • ✔ ΔH (overall energy change)
  • ✔ Position of equilibrium
  • ✘ Activation energy (they reduce it)

08.3 Calculate Rate

Calculate the rate at 60 seconds.

Reveal Calculation

Model Answer

Estimate from graph:

At 60 s → mass lost ≈ 1.20 g

At 50 s → mass lost ≈ 1.10 g

Rate ≈ change in mass / change in time

Rate ≈ (1.20 − 1.10) g ÷ (60 − 50) s

Rate ≈ 0.10 ÷ 10

Rate ≈ 0.01 g/s

Standard form: 1.0 × 10⁻² g/s

Exam Tip

Pick two points ON the line around 60 s (not the origin). Show working clearly for full marks.

08.4 Temperature Effect

Explain the effect of increasing temperature.

Reveal Model Answer (3 marks)

Model Answer

  1. Increasing temperature gives particles more kinetic energy.
  2. More particles collide with enough energy to react.
  3. More frequent successful collisions → faster rate.

Exam Tip

Always include the words:

  • ✔ kinetic energy
  • ✔ frequent collisions
  • ✔ successful / effective collisions
Q09

Ammonia & Haber Process

09.1 Temperature Choice

Why use a high temperature?

Reveal Answer

Model Answer

To increase the rate of reaction by giving particles more energy so collisions are more frequent and successful.

Extra note for higher marks: Even though high temperature favours the endothermic direction, it speeds up both forward and reverse reactions.

Exam Tip

Rate ↑ Yield ↓. Industry chooses a compromise.

09.2 Pressure Choice

Why use as low a pressure as possible?

Reveal Answer

Model Answer

To reduce cost and keep the process safe.

Higher-mark version: High pressure increases yield but requires expensive, strong equipment and is dangerous → compromise pressure used.

Revision Tip

Higher pressure = higher yield but high cost & risk.

Effect of catalyst on equilibrium position.

Reveal Answer

✔ No effect on equilibrium position

Explanation: Catalysts speed up both directions equally → do not affect yield.
